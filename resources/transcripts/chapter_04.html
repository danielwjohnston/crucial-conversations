Start with Heart

How to stay focused on what you really want

Core Message

The first principle of dialogue: Start with Heart

Work on me first, us second. The most skilled communicators begin by getting their own motives right.

When conversations get tough, our motives shift from solving the problem to winning, punishing, or keeping the peace . We need to notice and correct this shift.

The Third Option

What Do I Really Want?

Three critical questions to ask yourself:

For me?

What do I want for myself from this conversation?

For them?

What do I want for the other person?

For the relationship?

What do I want for the relationship between us?

Then ask: How would I behave if I really wanted these results?

How Motives Shift

Under stress, our motives shift without us noticing:

What We Start Wanting

  • To solve the problem
  • To understand each other
  • To strengthen the relationship

What We Shift To

  • To win the argument
  • To punish the other person
  • To avoid conflict at all costs

The shift happens fast. Notice it, name it, and return to your real purpose.

Dan's Digest #1

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

Motive shifts: Under stress, we shift from “solve the problem” to “win the argument,” “punish them,” or “keep the peace at all costs.” The shift is fast and unconscious.

The three questions: What do I want for me? For them? For the relationship? These re-anchor you on your real purpose.

Work on me first: You can't control others, but you can control your own motives and behavior. That's where dialogue begins.

Key Points

  • The shift from “solve” to “win” happens in seconds
  • Notice it, name it, return to purpose
  • The best at dialogue check their motives constantly

Dan's Digest #2

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

The Fool's Choice: The belief that you must choose between honesty and peace. Skilled communicators reject this and look for AND—both honest and respectful.

AND statements: Force your brain to search for creative solutions. “I want to be honest about my concerns AND respectful of your perspective.”

Why it works: When you hold both goals simultaneously, you find paths that either/or thinking hides.

Key Points

  • The Fool's Choice feels convincing because either/or is simpler
  • AND requires creativity and courage
  • Write your AND statement before the conversation, not during
