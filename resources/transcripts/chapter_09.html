Dan's Digest #1

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

AMPP tools: Ask to get things rolling, Mirror when words and tone don't match, Paraphrase to show understanding, Prime when getting nowhere. Each tool goes deeper into the other person's path.

Be curious: When people become furious, we need to become curious. Ask “Why would a reasonable person say this?” instead of “What's wrong with them?”

Retrace the path: We see their action (yelling/silence) first. Help them trace back to the facts and stories driving their emotions.

Key Points

  • Every sentence has a history—if you don't get to the facts, you're just arguing about emotions
  • Priming takes courage but works when nothing else does
  • Most people will reciprocate if you listen sincerely first

Dan's Digest #2

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

Violent agreement: Two people who agree on 90% fight over the 10% they differ on. Start with what you agree on, then build.

ABC approach: Agree where you can, Build by adding missing info, Compare when you truly differ. Never say “you're wrong”—say “I see it differently.”

Set expectations: “I'll listen with an open mind. Would you then be willing to hear my view?” Most people reciprocate.

Key Points

  • Don't turn agreement into argument over details
  • Use STATE skills when it's your turn to share
  • Exploring their path—even the nasty parts—gets to the real issue
