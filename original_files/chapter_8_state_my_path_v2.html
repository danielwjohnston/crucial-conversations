S — Share Your Facts

  • Start with the most observable, least controversial data.
  • Facts build safety and reduce argument about intent.
  • Use time stamps, counts, and quotes verbatim.
Facilitator Notes

T — Tell Your Story (tentatively)

  • Move from facts to your tentative conclusion.
  • Signal tentativeness: “I’m beginning to wonder…”, “I’m concerned that…”.
  • Never start with the story—earn it with facts first.

A — Ask for Others’ Paths

  • Invite their facts, stories, and concerns.
  • Use openers: “How do you see it?” “What am I missing?”
  • Look for signs of safety dropping; restore it.

T — Talk Tentatively (Goldilocks Language)

Understated

  • “There might be a small issue.” (when it’s critical)
  • “I’m not sure but…” (when you are sure)

Overstated

  • “You always ignore protocols.”
  • “This is a disaster because of you.”

Just‑Right

  • “When X, Y happened (facts). I’m concluding Z; am I missing something?”
  • “I’m concerned we skipped step 3 twice today—can we review together?”
Facilitator Notes
Have the room craft a ‘just‑right’ version for a live example.

E — Encourage Testing

  • Explicitly invite disconfirming data.
  • Make it safe to disagree: “If you see it differently, I want to hear it.”
  • Reward those who correct you—don’t punish candor.

STATE — String It Through (One Scenario)

  1. Share Facts: “Mon/Tue break ran :22 long; three cues late.”
  2. Tell Story: “I’m concerned the checklist is skipped under load.”
  3. Ask: “What are you seeing?”
  4. Talk Tentatively: “I could be off here…”
  5. Encourage Testing: “If I’m missing something, please correct me.”
Facilitator Notes
Walk this once slowly; then have pairs try it with their own scenario.
