The Inevitable Hand

As we meet with Crucial Conversations trainers around the world, they report that at the end of a class, there is inevitably someone who raises a hand and says something in the form of "Yeah, but..."

Common Examples:

  • "Yeah, but my boss would never respond like that"
  • "Yeah, but my teenager could ignore a tsunami"
  • "Yeah, but what if I'm not carrying my training manual when the crucial moment hits?"

People can think of a dozen reasons why the skills don't apply to their challenges.

The Truth About Tough Cases

In truth, the dialogue skills we've shared apply to just about any problem you can imagine.

However, some situations are more difficult than others, and some people are harder to work with.

This chapter addresses the toughest cases:

  • When emotions run extremely high
  • When stakes are at their highest
  • When people seem impossible to reach
  • When you feel completely powerless

Tough Case #1: The Powerless Person

"Yeah, but I have no power in this situation."

"I'm just a junior employee/my spouse controls everything/my kids won't listen to me."

The Reality:

You always have influence. The question is how to use it effectively.

  • Focus on what you CAN control (your responses, your preparation)
  • Build influence through credibility and relationships
  • Choose your timing and approach strategically
  • Use natural consequences rather than force

Tough Case #2: The Impossible Person

"Yeah, but this person is impossible to talk to."

"They're narcissistic/toxic/always defensive/never listen."

The Strategy:

  • Start with extreme safety - Double your efforts to make it safe
  • Master your stories - Challenge your labels about them
  • Look for patterns - What triggers their behavior?
  • Use contrasting - Clarify what you DON'T want
  • Set boundaries - Know when to walk away

Tough Case #3: The Life-or-Death Situation

"Yeah, but this is too important to get wrong."

"Someone could die/we could lose the company/our relationship could end."

The Approach:

  • Prepare extensively - This is when preparation matters most
  • Bring in allies - Don't go it alone when stakes are highest
  • Use facts and data - Ground the conversation in reality
  • Focus on mutual purpose - Emphasize shared goals
  • Have a backup plan - Know your Plan B if dialogue fails

Tough Case #4: The No-Time Scenario

"Yeah, but I don't have time to prepare.

"The crucial moment hits without warning/I'm too busy/it's happening right now!"

The Reality:

Most crucial conversations ARE predictable if you know what to look for.

  • Scan for warning signs - Learn to spot crucial moments early
  • Have go-to phrases - Prepare key lines in advance
  • Use the pause button - "Can we talk about this later?"
  • Master the basics - Start with Heart, Make It Safe
  • Practice in low-stakes situations - Build muscle memory

The Universal Principle

Across all tough cases, one principle remains constant:

The skills work, but they must be applied with more:

  • Skill - Better execution of the fundamentals
  • Preparation - More thought and planning
  • Patience - More time and persistence
  • Courage - More willingness to try and fail
  • Support - More allies and resources
