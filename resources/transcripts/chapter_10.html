Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

The pen metaphor: Your pen is the power to define your worth. When you hold it, others' opinions are information. When you've handed it away, any feedback can feel devastating.

Why feedback hurts: The pain doesn't track with content or delivery. It tracks with who holds your pen. Carmen's uncle's comment stuck for decades because she'd given him authority over her worth.

We're born holding our pen. Babies don't fret over others' opinions. We hand it away gradually as we seek external validation.

Key Points

  • If you live by the compliment, you'll die by the criticism
  • The biggest variable isn't the message—it's who held your pen in that moment
  • Personal security comes from within, not from others' approval
