Make It Safe

How to Make It Safe to Talk About Almost Anything

"A word fitly spoken is like apples of gold in baskets of silver."
— Proverbs 25:11

The Promise of Safety

The last chapter contained a promise: If you spot safety risks as they happen, you can step out of the conversation, build safety, and then find a way to talk about just about anything with just about anyone.

In this chapter, we'll fulfill that promise by teaching what it takes to create and restore safety.

Safety is the foundation of dialogue. Without it, people either shut down or lash out.

Think About It:

When do you feel safest sharing your thoughts? When do you feel most defensive?

Meet Oba and Mari

Let's eavesdrop on a crucial conversation between a couple named Oba and Mari.

The Context:

  • Oba is a chef
  • Mari is a project manager for a global supply chain company
  • The last year has been hard - recession triggered restructuring
  • Mari took on additional responsibilities while downsizing
  • She's been working 70-hour weeks for months

The stress is affecting their relationship, and they need to talk about it.

Two Conditions of Safety

People feel safe when two conditions are met:

1. Mutual Purpose

They believe that you care about their goals and interests as much as your own.

  • Shared objectives
  • Common ground
  • Win-win mindset

2. Mutual Respect

They believe that you respect them as a person, even if you disagree with their position.

  • Dignity preserved
  • Value acknowledged
  • Worth affirmed

When Safety Is at Risk

Warning Signs:

  • Insults - "You're so selfish!"
  • Accusations - "You never think about anyone but yourself"
  • Contempt - Eye-rolling, sarcasm, dismissive tone
  • Defensiveness - "That's not fair!"
  • Stonewalling - Silence, withdrawal

When you see these signs, STOP. Don't continue until you restore safety.

The first rule of crucial conversations: Fix safety first, then address the issue.

How to Build Safety

Technique 1: Apologize When Appropriate

A sincere apology can restore safety instantly.

"I'm sorry I came across that way. That wasn't my intention."

Technique 2: Use Contrasting

Clarify what you DON'T mean, then state what you DO mean.

"I don't want you to think I'm ungrateful for your hard work. I do want to talk about how we can balance our family needs with your career demands."

Technique 3: Create Mutual Purpose

Find shared goals and commit to finding solutions that work for everyone.

"We both want what's best for our family. Let's figure this out together."

Step-by-Step Safety Building

Step 1: Commit to Seek Mutual Purpose

Start with the belief that you can find a solution that works for both of you.

Step 2: Recognize the Purpose Behind the Strategy

Look beyond positions to understand underlying needs and concerns.

Step 3: Invent a Mutual Purpose

Brainstorm solutions that address both sets of concerns.

Step 4: Brainstorm New Strategies

With shared purpose established, explore multiple ways to achieve it.

Safety isn't about avoiding conflict—it's about creating conditions where conflict can be productive.

The Safety Toolkit

Quick Safety Phrases:

  • "I want to make sure we both feel heard in this conversation."
  • "My intention isn't to criticize, but to understand."
  • "Can we step back and make sure we're on the same page?"
  • "I value your perspective, even if we see things differently."
  • "Let's find a solution that works for both of us."

Remember:

When safety is restored, you can talk about anything. When safety is missing, you can't talk about anything productively.

Making it safe isn't a technique—it's a mindset. It's about genuinely caring about the other person while staying true to yourself.
