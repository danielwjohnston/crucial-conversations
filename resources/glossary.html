Crucial Conversations Glossary

Quick-reference definitions for every key term, acronym, and framework

Core Acronyms

STATE Ch 8
Share your facts • Tell your story • Ask for others' paths • Talk tentatively • Encourage testing
The five skills for speaking persuasively, not abrasively. Start with facts (least controversial), share your story tentatively, then invite the other person's perspective.
CPR Ch 3
Content • Pattern • Relationship
Three levels of every issue. Content = the specific event. Pattern = it keeps happening. Relationship = trust or respect is affected. Choose the right level before you speak.
AMPP Ch 9
Ask • Mirror • Paraphrase • Prime
Four listening tools to explore others' paths. Ask to express interest, Mirror to acknowledge emotions, Paraphrase to restate and confirm, Prime to guess when they're stuck.
ABC Ch 9
Agree • Build • Compare
How to respond after listening. Agree where you genuinely agree, Build by adding what's missing, Compare where your views differ â€” without suggesting the other person is wrong.
CRIB Ch 7
Commit to seek mutual purpose • Recognize the purpose behind the strategy • Invent a mutual purpose • Brainstorm new strategies
Used when you and the other person have different purposes. Step back from strategies to find the underlying goals you share.
CURE Ch 10
Collect yourself • Understand • Recover • Engage
How to hear tough feedback without losing your pen. Breathe and name your feelings, be curious, ask for time if needed, then find the gold and commit to action.
WWWF Ch 11
Who • does What • by When • how will you Follow up
The assignment framework that turns dialogue into action. Assign a specific person, spell out deliverables, set a deadline, and build in a check-in. Document it.

Key Concepts

Pool of Shared Meaning Ch 2
The collection of all meaning openly shared by everyone in a conversation. Bigger pool = better decisions, stronger commitment, more unified action. The goal of dialogue is to get all relevant meaning into the pool.
Path to Action Ch 5
See/Hear → Tell a Story → Feel → Act
The sequence from observation to behavior. We see or hear something, tell ourselves a story about it, generate feelings from that story, and then act. The story is where we have leverage.
Fool's Choice Ch 4
The false belief that you must choose between honesty and peace. Skilled communicators reject the either/or and ask: "How can I be 100% honest AND 100% respectful?"
Mutual Purpose Ch 7
The first condition of safety. Others believe you care about their goals and interests, not just your own. Without it, people suspect your motives and resist dialogue.
Mutual Respect Ch 7
The second condition of safety. Others believe you respect them as a person, even during disagreement. Without it, conversations become about defending dignity rather than solving problems.
Contrast Ch 7
"I don't want [negative]. I do want [positive]."
A don't/do statement that addresses misunderstood intent. Use it to restore safety when someone has taken what you said the wrong way. The don't addresses their concern; the do clarifies your real purpose.
Retake Your Pen Ch 10
Your "pen" is the power to define your worth. When you hold it, others' opinions are information. When you've handed it away, any feedback can feel devastating. Retake it by grounding your worth internally, not in others' approval.

Behavioral Patterns

Silence Ch 6
Masking • Avoiding • Withdrawing
Withholding meaning from the pool. Any act to purposely keep information out of the conversation. Phrases like "It's fine" or "Whatever you think" are signs of silence.
Violence Ch 6
Controlling • Labeling • Attacking
Forcing meaning into the pool. Any act to compel others to accept your point of view. Phrases like "That's ridiculous" or "You always do this" are signs of violence.
Three Clever Stories Ch 5
Victim • Villain • Helpless
Self-justifying stories that let us feel good about behaving badly. Victim: "It's not my fault." Villain: "It's all your fault." Helpless: "There's nothing I can do."
Lag Time Ch 2
The delay between when a problem occurs and when someone talks about it. During the delay, stories and resentment build. Shortening lag time is one of the highest-leverage things you can do.

Decision Methods

Command Ch 11
Decisions made without involving others. Use when stakes are low, it's not worth everyone's time, or someone has clear authority.
Consult Ch 11
Input is gathered from others, then a subset decides. Use when many are affected, expertise is needed, but not everyone must agree.
Vote Ch 11
An agreed-upon percentage swings the decision. Use when efficiency matters and the group can support any outcome.
Consensus Ch 11
Everyone comes to agreement. Use when high-stakes, everyone must genuinely support the decision. Saves the most commitment but costs the most time.

During Dialogue

STATE My Path (Ch 8) — Share, Tell, Ask, Talk tentatively, Encourage

How to Finish
Retake Your Pen (Ch 10) — CURE to hear feedback
