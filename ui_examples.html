Optional Deepening Prompts

If time allows, choose 1–2.

What are the three elements that make a conversation crucial?

What is dialogue, and what is the Pool of Shared Meaning?

Tone: supportive, optional, curriculum-native.

Wrap-Up Round

One minute each — pick one prompt to answer.

What’s one crucial conversation you want to handle better?

What does “lag time” cost you right now?

Tone: fast-paced close, everyone participates.

Question Bank (Use as Needed)

Choose based on your group’s energy and time.

How do we typically handle crucial conversations?

Why are we often on our worst behavior?

Tone: facilitator toolset, not “official.”

Bonus Questions (If We Have Time)

Skip if we’re tight — these are for extra depth.

What are some examples of typical crucial conversations?

What is lag time, and why is it harmful?

Tone: transparent, permission to skip.

Look Back / Look Ahead

Close the loop and tee up next week.

Look back: Which of the three elements shows up most for you?

Look ahead: Next chapter we’ll focus on choosing the right topic.

Tone: cohesive transition into next chapter.

Coach’s Notes (Dan’s Digest)

Quick Rescue

If the group is quiet, ask for a concrete example first (work/home). Then map it to opinions, stakes, emotions.

What a strong answer includes

  • Names the 3 elements clearly (opinions, stakes, emotions)
  • Gives a real example from work or home
  • Notes the tendency toward silence/violence under stress
  • Shifts goal from “win” to “grow the pool”
