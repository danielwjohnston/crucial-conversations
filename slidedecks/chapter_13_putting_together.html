Putting It All Together

Tools for preparing and learning

The Complete Model

Before You Open Your Mouth

  • Start with Heart — What do I really want?
  • Choose Your Topic (CPR) — Am I addressing the right issue?

How to Open Your Mouth

  • Learn to Look — Is it safe? Am I in silence or violence?
  • Make It Safe — Mutual Purpose + Mutual Respect
  • Master My Stories — Facts → Story → Feeling → Action
  • STATE My Path — Share facts, Tell story, Ask, Talk tentatively, Encourage testing
  • Explore Others' Paths — Ask, Mirror, Paraphrase, Prime

How to Finish

  • Move to Action — Decide how to decide + WWWF

How to Keep Improving

Skill development requires practice and accountability:

  • Pick one skill to focus on for the next 30 days
  • Find a practice partner—someone who will give you honest feedback
  • Debrief conversations: What went well? What would you do differently?
  • Re-read the chapter that addresses your biggest gap
  • Be patient: You're building new neural pathways. It takes time.

The Preparation Tool

Before any crucial conversation, write down:

1. What do I really want? (for me, for them, for the relationship)

2. What are the facts? (observable, verifiable)

3. What's my story? (the meaning I'm adding)

4. How will I make it safe? (contrasting statement, good intent)

5. How will I move to action? (WWWF)

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

Common “aha” moments: Many people are surprised by Master My Stories (realizing they make themselves angry) or Make It Safe (safety is about intent, not content). Others find AMPP listening skills transformative.

Hardest to apply: Most people struggle with skills that go against their natural tendencies—the silent types struggle with STATE, the forceful types with listening.

Key Points

  • The complete model is a toolkit, not a script—use what you need
  • Start with the skill that addresses your biggest gap
  • Awareness is the first step—you can't fix what you don't see

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

Preparation is power: The five-step prep tool (Want, Facts, Story, Safety, Action) takes 5 minutes and dramatically improves outcomes.

Start small: Don't pick your hardest conversation first. Build confidence with medium-stakes situations, then work up.

Debrief is essential: Every conversation is practice. Debrief what worked and what you'd change. Skill comes from reflection, not just repetition.

Key Points

  • Write it down—preparation on paper beats preparation in your head
  • One skill at a time for 30 days builds lasting habits
  • You're building new neural pathways—be patient with yourself
