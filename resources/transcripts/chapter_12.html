Core Message

The skills aren't different for tough cases

Tough Case: The Sensitive Topic

Your partner, friend, or coworker reacts so strongly you've stopped raising issues

  • Start with Heart : What do you really want? (Not to avoid, but to resolve)
  • Contrast : ‚ÄúI don't want you to think I'm attacking you. I do want us to be able to talk about things that matter.‚Äù
  • STATE your path: Share facts, tell your story tentatively, ask for their view
  • Make it safe: If they blow up, step out of content and rebuild safety

Tough Case: The Accountability Gap

Your direct report, child, or partner waits to be told

  • Choose your topic (CPR ): Is this about Content (the task), Pattern (it keeps happening), or Relationship (trust)?
  • Describe the gap: ‚ÄúWe agreed on X. What I observed was Y. I'd like to understand what happened.‚Äù
  • Make it motivating: Help them see the natural consequences
  • Follow up with WWWF : Clear assignments prevent drift

Tough Case: The Authority Gap

  • Use tentative language: Share facts and your story without absolute certainty

Dan's Digest #1

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

The avoidance trap: The conversations we avoid are usually the ones that matter most. The cost of NOT having them compounds over time.

CPR helps you choose the right conversation: Content (the specific issue), Pattern (it keeps happening), or Relationship (trust is eroding). Most people stay at Content when they should escalate to Pattern or Relationship.

Courage + Skill: The skills don't change for tough cases. What changes is the amount of courage required.

Key Points

  • The moment you think “yeah, but” is exactly when you need the skills most
  • Contrast is your best friend in sensitive conversations
  • Start with Heart before every tough conversation

Dan's Digest #2

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

Common skip points: Most people skip Start with Heart (they jump in reactive) or Master My Stories (they believe their story is fact). Slowing down at these steps changes everything downstream.

The universal pattern works everywhere: Whether it's a sensitive topic, an accountability gap, or an authority gap—the same framework applies.

Key Points

  • Every “yeah, but” follows the same pattern
  • The step you skip is usually the one that matters most
  • Practice in low-stakes situations builds skill for high-stakes ones
