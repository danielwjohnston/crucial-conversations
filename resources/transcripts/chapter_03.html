Choose Your Topic

How to be sure you hold the right conversation

Core Message

The biggest mistake in crucial conversations is choosing the wrong topic

We often argue about a specific event when the real issue is a recurring pattern or a damaged relationship.

Before you open your mouth, make sure you're having the right conversation.

Three Skills for Choosing Your Topic

1. Unbundle

Dan's Digest #1

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

CPR: Content is the specific event. Pattern is the recurring behavior. Relationship is the impact on trust and respect. Most people stay at Content when they should escalate.

The biggest mistake: Choosing the wrong topic. You can have the most skillful conversation in the world, but if you're addressing the wrong issue, nothing changes.

Rule of thumb: If you've had the conversation before, you're probably at the wrong level. Go deeper.

Key Points

  • Unbundle first—most frustrations are multiple issues tangled together
  • Ask “What do I really want?” to find the right level
  • If you can't state it in one sentence, you haven't simplified enough

Dan's Digest #2

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

Groundhog Day: When you keep having the same conversation, it's a sign you're stuck at Content. Escalate to Pattern (“this keeps happening”) or Relationship (“this is affecting my trust”).

Naming it directly: “I want to talk about the pattern I'm seeing, not just this one instance.” This shifts the conversation to the real issue.

Key Points

  • Disproportionate emotion = deeper issue below the surface
  • Whack-a-mole means you're treating symptoms, not root cause
  • When in doubt, go one level deeper
