Silence and Violence

Two unhealthy paths when people feel unsafe

Silence

Withholding meaning from the pool

  • Masking: Sarcasm, sugarcoating
  • Avoiding: Steering away from topics
  • Withdrawing: Pulling out entirely

Violence

Forcing meaning into the pool

  • Controlling: Dominating, interrupting
  • Labeling: Dismissing with stereotypes
  • Attacking: Belittling, threatening

Dan's Digest #1

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #1)

Dual processing: The key skill is learning to watch both content (what's being said) and conditions (how it's being said) simultaneously. Most people get so absorbed in the argument that they miss the warning signs.

Physical cues: Your body often knows before your brain does. Stomach tightening, heart racing, dry mouth—these are your early warning system. Learn to treat them as signals to step back and observe.

The sooner you catch it: The earlier you notice a conversation has turned crucial, the less damage is done and the easier it is to restore dialogue.

Key Points

  • People don't become defensive because of what you say—they become defensive when they don't feel safe
  • Safety ≠ comfort. Crucial conversations are inherently uncomfortable. The question is whether meaning is flowing.
  • When you notice you're not in dialogue, that awareness alone re-engages your higher reasoning

Dan's Digest #2

Dan's Digest

Answer Key (Discussion Break #2)

Silence forms: Masking (sarcasm, sugarcoating), Avoiding (steering away from topics), Withdrawing (pulling out entirely). All restrict the flow of meaning.

Violence forms: Controlling (dominating, interrupting), Labeling (dismissing with stereotypes), Attacking (belittling, threatening). All try to force meaning into the pool.

Your style: Most people have a default pattern. Knowing yours is the first step to changing it. The Style Under Stress assessment helps you identify your go-to moves.

Key Points

  • When someone attacks or withdraws, think “they feel unsafe” not “they're a jerk”
  • Be curious instead of angry or frightened—that shift alone changes the dynamic
  • Virtual communication limits your data stream; choose higher-bandwidth media for crucial conversations
