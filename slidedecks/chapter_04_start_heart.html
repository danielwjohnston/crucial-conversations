How to Stay Focused on What You Really Want

The Challenge of Dialogue

Now that you know what you want to talk about, it's time to turn to the how of dialogue.

How do you encourage the flow of meaning when you're:

  • In the thrall of strong emotions
  • Talking about things that matter deeply to you
  • With those who disagree vehemently

Given that most people's style is based on long-standing habits, it'll require a lot of effort.

People Can Change

The truth is, people can change.

We've taught these conversation skills to millions around the world and have seen dramatic improvements in results and relationships.

But it requires work. You can't simply highlight an inspiring paragraph in a book and expect to behave differently the next day.

Change requires deliberate practice and conscious effort.

Start with Heart: The First Skill

This is where it all begins. Before you open your mouth, you must know what you really want.

The Core Principle:

Focus on what you really want.

When you're in the middle of a crucial conversation, it's easy to lose sight of your goals.

  • Emotions take over
  • Ego gets involved
  • Winning becomes more important than resolving

The Three Questions

Before and during any crucial conversation, ask yourself:

1. What do I really want for myself?

What are my real goals and objectives?

2. What do I really want for others?

What outcomes would benefit the other person?

3. What do I really want for the relationship?

How can I strengthen our connection?

Return to Purpose

When you find yourself drifting away from what you really want:

  1. Stop and step back - Take a mental pause
  2. Ask the three questions - Reconnect with your true goals
  3. Clarify your purpose - Get specific about what you want
  4. Choose your response - Act in alignment with your goals

Practice Exercise:

Think of a recent difficult conversation. What did you want for yourself? For others? For the relationship? Did you stay focused on these goals?

Common Traps That Derail Us

Trap 1: Wanting to Win

We shift from dialogue to debate, from seeking truth to seeking victory.

Trap 2: Seeking Revenge

We want to punish or hurt the other person for past wrongs.

Trap 3: Wanting to Be Safe

We avoid the real issues to prevent conflict or discomfort.

When you catch yourself in these traps, return to your purpose.

The Power of Clarity

When you're clear about what you really want:

  • Your emotions become more manageable
  • Your choices become more deliberate
  • Your communication becomes more effective
  • Your relationships become stronger

The Transformation:

From reactive and emotional to purposeful and constructive.

Self-Reflection:

What crucial conversation are you facing? What do you really want for yourself, others, and the relationship?
